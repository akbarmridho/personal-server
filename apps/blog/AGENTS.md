# Blog Posts Writing Guide

## Writing Style

- Conversational, self-deprecating, raw. Writes like talking to a friend, not presenting to an audience.
- Freely mixes English with casual internet-speak. Doesn't care about grammar perfection — fragments, run-ons, lowercase mid-sentence, all intentional.
- Self-roasts constantly: "dumber than monkey?", "maybe yes", "it's supit", "i don't know lol".
- Uses rhetorical questions as transitions: "So, I was dumber than monkey?", "can we utilize LLMs in our process?"
- Parenthetical asides for honesty: "(maybe yes)", "(almost) exactly the same", "(doesn't mean profitable yes)".
- Builds momentum with repetition: "down and down and down", "loop after loop".
- Drops punchlines dry: "There goes the MSCI freeze and the rest is history.", "No-as-a-Service."
- Mixes technical credibility with self-awareness — names real tools/concepts but immediately undercuts any flex: "I'm not a quant and not good at math anyway."
- Bullet points for structured ideas, flowing prose for narrative/story sections.
- Closes sections with honest, unpolished takes rather than neat conclusions: "it is what it is and at least now its usable"
- Uses em dashes and ellipses naturally, not formally.
- Never sounds like a blog template. No "In this article, we will explore..." energy.
- Metaphors are punchy and original: "smart goldfish", "paying for a genius that forgets everything every hour".
- Indonesian context bleeds through naturally (IHSG, IDX, bandar, Stockbit) without over-explaining for western audiences.

## Content Rules

- This is an Astro blog using markdown content collections.
- Posts live in `src/content/posts/{slug}/index.md` with co-located images.
- Reference images with relative paths: `![alt](./image.png)`
- Frontmatter schema: title, description, pubDate, author, tags, image (optional), draft (optional).
- Write in the author's voice above. Do not sanitize, formalize, or "improve" the tone.
