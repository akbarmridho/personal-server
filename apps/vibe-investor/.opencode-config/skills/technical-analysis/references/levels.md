# Levels

## Objective

Map meaningful decision zones first, then interpret price relative to those zones.

Levels are context.
They are not trade permission by themselves.

## Horizontal Core

- levels are zones, not single lines
- higher-timeframe and repeatedly respected zones matter more
- first retest is strongest
- repeated tests weaken a level
- broken support or resistance can flip role after acceptance
- use close-based confirmation, not wick-only breaches, for acceptance logic
- recently broken resistance that is accepted and then holds as nearby support still counts as `accepted_above_resistance`
- map first and trade second

## Zone Construction

Use one method consistently per analysis:

- fixed-width zone
- ATR-width zone
- wick-to-body reaction zone

Keep the map small and decision-oriented.
Treat proximity against the full zone width, not only the midpoint.
Allow a small buffer around the zone so near-edge acceptance is not mislabeled as noise.

## Trace Requirements

- key support zones
- key resistance zones
- touched dates or reaction periods when relevant
- role-reversal note when relevant

## Moving Average Context

Moving averages are dynamic context, not standalone entry signals.

### Baseline MA

Use the lean baseline:

- `21EMA`
- `50SMA`
- `200SMA`

Read them as:

- support
- resistance
- noise

### Adaptive MA

Use adaptive MA only when:

- the symbol shows repeated respect to a specific rhythm
- the setup is timing-sensitive
- the baseline MA context is not enough

Adaptive MA refines the read.
It does not replace baseline regime context.

Chart usage:

- `hybrid` keeps the baseline stack and highlights the chosen `SMA{n}`
- `baseline` keeps `21EMA`, `50SMA`, `200SMA`

## Time-Based And Round Levels

Use when materially relevant:

- daily open
- weekly open
- monthly open
- round-number levels

These are context enhancers, not standalone triggers.

## Value-Area And VPVR Confluence

Use VPVR and value-area structure to strengthen level quality.

Core ideas:

- `POC` is a fair-value magnet
- `HVN` is a reaction zone
- `LVN` is a fast-travel zone
- value-area acceptance helps distinguish acceptance from rejection

Use VPVR as confluence with:

- structure
- horizontal zones
- liquidity behavior
- MA context
- time or round levels

## Practical Mapping Order

1. daily support and resistance
2. structural swing highs and lows
3. value-area references
4. baseline MA context
5. time-based and round levels when relevant

## Trace Requirements

- POC / VAH / VAL when used
- major HVN / LVN when used
- acceptance or rejection state
- confluence note when VPVR materially affects the level read
