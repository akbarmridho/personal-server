# Indonesia Gold Playbook

Use this playbook only when gold exposure is thesis-critical. It is a doctrine reference, not a stored research report.

## Core Split

IDX gold exposure usually falls into four lanes:

| Lane | What drives results | Main checks |
|---|---|---|
| Exploration / pre-production | resource quality, permitting, funding, build-out credibility | resource quality, development funding, timeline risk |
| Gold mining | production, grade, recovery, cash cost, reserve life, gold price | ounces, grade, recovery, strip ratio when relevant, cash cost, reserve replacement |
| Refining / fabrication | throughput, spread, working capital, distribution, trust/brand | volume, spread, inventory risk, brand/liquidity premium |
| Copper-linked by-product gold | copper economics first, gold credit second | parent metal cycle, by-product accounting, smelter/refinery path |

Do not analyze all "gold stocks" as if they share the same economics.

## Business-Model Split

### Mining model

- Earnings sensitivity to gold price is high.
- Economics depend on production volume, grade, recovery, strip ratio or mining difficulty, and cash cost.
- Capex burden is heavy and reserve depletion is a constant issue.

### Trading / refining / fabrication model

- Earnings sensitivity to gold price level is lower than investors often assume.
- Economics depend more on volume, spread, turnover, inventory management, and brand / distribution strength.
- Working-capital discipline matters more than reserve life.

## Gold From Gold Mines vs Gold From Copper Mines

| Topic | Gold mine | Copper mine by-product gold |
|---|---|---|
| Primary economic driver | gold price and mine economics | copper economics first, gold as credit |
| Feedstock | ore / dore | anode slime or copper-related processing stream |
| Cost read | gold cash cost is meaningful | standalone gold cash cost is often not meaningful |
| Valuation trap | overpaying for ounces without reserve quality | treating gold output as if it had pure-play gold sensitivity |

If a company produces gold as a by-product, do not model it like a pure-play gold miner.

## Reserve And Resource Logic

- `resources` indicate geological potential.
- `reserves` indicate the portion currently economic to mine.
- Reserve quality matters more than headline resource size.

Key reserve questions:

- reserve life at current production
- grade quality
- reserve replacement credibility
- sensitivity to gold price and cost inflation
- permitting and license duration

Reserve growth is not always discovery quality. It can come from higher gold prices, lower cost assumptions, or technical changes.

## Ore And Processing Logic

### Oxide vs sulfide

| Topic | Oxide | Sulfide |
|---|---|---|
| Typical location | nearer surface | deeper / more complex |
| Processing difficulty | easier | harder |
| Cost profile | lower | higher |
| Typical implication | lower capex / simpler flow sheet | higher capex / pre-treatment risk |

### Heap leach vs CIL

| Topic | Heap leach | CIL |
|---|---|---|
| Best fit | lower-grade ore | medium / higher-grade ore |
| Capex | lower | higher |
| Recovery | lower | higher |
| Throughput speed | slower | faster |
| Main use | cheaper extraction on lower-value material | better yield when grade justifies spend |

Do not compare miners on production alone. Compare the grade-cost-recovery combination.

## Refining And Fabrication Logic

For refiners and fabricators, focus on:

- throughput growth
- spread / fee economics
- inventory and working-capital management
- end-market demand
- product trust and liquidity
- distribution reach

For branded bullion, an accreditation or trust premium can matter. In Indonesia, LBMA-recognized output can support stronger market acceptance and liquidity.

## Key Metrics By Lane

### Gold miners

- production ounces
- reserve life
- grade
- recovery rate
- cash cost / AISC when available
- capex burden
- reserve replacement ratio

### Refiners / fabricators

- throughput
- inventory turns
- working-capital intensity
- spread / fee stability
- brand and distribution quality

### Copper-linked producers

- copper production and realized copper economics
- gold credit contribution
- smelter / refinery path
- whether gold meaningfully changes the valuation case

## Practical IDX Classification

Use this classification before making a call:

- pure-play producer
- mixed miner with gold as one segment
- pre-production developer
- refiner / fabricator / retailer
- copper-linked by-product producer

Mixed groups should not receive pure-play gold multiples without adjustment.

## Red Flags

- large resource headlines with weak reserve conversion
- production growth story without funding visibility
- rising output but worsening cost or recovery
- valuation anchored to company guidance without build credibility
- treating refinery/fabrication names like direct gold-price bets
- treating by-product producers like pure-play gold miners
- overstating resource size without license, permitting, or economics clarity

## Minimum Questions Before Using Gold Exposure In A Thesis

1. What exact lane is this company in?
2. Is gold the main business, one segment, or a by-product?
3. What are the true earnings drivers?
4. Does valuation match the lane?
5. What would break the thesis: gold price, grade, reserve life, funding, throughput, spread, or parent-metal economics?
