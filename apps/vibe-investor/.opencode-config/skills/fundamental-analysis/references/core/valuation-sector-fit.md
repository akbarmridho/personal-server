# Valuation Sector Fit

## Purpose

Use sector or business-model fit before selecting methods. The same multiple means different things across sectors because capital structure, payout behavior, asset transparency, regulation, and cycle position differ.

## Sector And Model Fit

| Sector or model | Stronger methods | Usually wrong or weak | What to verify first |
|---|---|---|---|
| Banks | `Justified P/B`, `DDM`, peer `P/E` | `EV/EBITDA`, generic `DCF` | `ROE`, `NPL`, `CKPN`, capital ratios, deposit franchise |
| Insurance | `P/B`, adjusted earnings, dividend lens | `EV/EBITDA` | reserve quality, investment marks, capital adequacy |
| Consumer compounders | `DCF`, `P/E`, `EV/EBITDA` | asset-based anchor as primary | moat, pricing power, reinvestment runway |
| Telecom / utilities | `DCF`, `EV/EBITDA` | simple `P/E` without debt context | capex, regulation, maturity, leverage |
| Property / REIT | `NAV`, `DDM` for REIT, `SOTP` when mixed | `P/E` as primary | appraisal realism, occupancy, debt, realization discount |
| Holding / conglomerate | `SOTP`, `NAV` when stakes dominate | single blended multiple | ownership stakes, holdco debt, overhead, tax leakage |
| Mining / coal / commodity | normalized `EV/EBITDA`, normalized `P/E`, reserve-led `NAV` | spot-year `P/E` | cycle position, strip price, reserve life, capex |
| Tech / growth | `EV/Revenue`, scenario `DCF`, `SOTP` for mixed models | `P/E`, `EV/EBITDA` before maturity | revenue quality, burn, dilution, path to margins |
| Asset-heavy industrial | `EV/EBITDA`, `DCF`, sometimes `NAV` | raw `P/B` without return context | maintenance capex, utilization, cycle position |

## Banks And Insurance

- Book value is meaningful because balance-sheet quality is central.
- Sustainable `ROE` explains deserved premium or discount to book.
- Dividend capacity matters because payout is constrained by regulation and capital generation.
- Cheap `P/B` is not attractive if asset quality is deteriorating.

## Cyclicals And Commodities

- Use normalized numbers across the cycle.
- Low `P/E` at the top of the cycle is often a warning, not a bargain.
- Reserve life, stripping ratio, realized prices, and replacement capex can matter as much as near-term earnings.

## Growth And Tech

- `EV/Revenue` is only defensible if revenue quality and future margins can be explained.
- Rule-of-40 style framing helps separate high-quality growth from low-quality growth.
- Scenario analysis is usually more honest than single-point precision.
- Dilution and funding path should sit inside the valuation case.

## Conglomerates And Holdings

- Use different methods for different segments.
- Apply ownership stakes correctly.
- Reduce gross segment value for holdco debt, tax leakage, and overhead.
- A governance or complexity discount may be justified even when segment values are sound.

## Macro Sensitivity By Type

| Business type | Most rate-sensitive | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Growth / long-duration | High | distant cash flows and premium multiples |
| Highly leveraged operating business | High | discount-rate and earnings pressure both hit |
| Banks | Mixed | `NIM` can improve, but asset quality and funding costs matter |
| REIT / property | High | rate sensitivity plus financing burden |
| Consumer defensives | Lower | cash flow stability and pricing power |
| Commodity producers | Mixed | macro matters through cycle and FX, not just discount rate |

## Practical Rule

Before using any multiple, ask:

- does this sector actually trade on this metric?
- does this metric map to how value is created here?
- what usually justifies premium vs discount inside this sector?
