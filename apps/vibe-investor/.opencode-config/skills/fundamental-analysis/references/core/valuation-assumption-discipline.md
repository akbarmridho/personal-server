# Valuation Assumption Discipline

## Philosophy

- Intrinsic value is an estimate of business value, not a precise answer.
- The right output is usually a range with explicit conditions, not one target number.
- Margin of Safety protects against model error, business deterioration, and analytical overconfidence.
- Price can deviate from value for long periods, but valuation still needs present-tense discipline.

## Range Discipline

- Prefer `bear / base / bull` framing or explicit sensitivity when uncertainty is high.
- Single-point estimates are acceptable only when the business economics are unusually stable.
- Wider uncertainty should produce wider valuation ranges, not more confident language.

## Core Assumption Rules

- Build from current operating facts before projecting improvement.
- Separate what is already visible from what must go right.
- Use explicit drivers: revenue growth, margin, reinvestment, working capital, leverage, payout.
- Tie long-run growth to business maturity and macro reality.
- Keep discount-rate logic explicit, especially in Indonesia where `CoE` and country risk matter materially.

## DCF Discipline

- `Terminal value` is usually the most fragile part of the model.
- Explain how much of total value comes from the terminal period.
- Keep terminal growth below discount rate.
- Use sensitivity tables when small changes in `WACC`, `CoE`, or `g` move value materially.
- If most value depends on distant assumptions with little present evidence, lower confidence sharply.

## Cyclical Normalization

- Do not treat peak margins or peak commodity prices as normal.
- Use normalized earnings, margins, and multiples across the cycle.
- If the company is being valued on trough conditions, explain what a normalized recovery actually looks like.
- Recency bias is especially dangerous in cyclicals.

## Multiple Discipline

- Match numerator and denominator correctly.
- Compare to peers only after checking business mix, geography, capital structure, and cycle position.
- High multiples can be justified by durability, moat, and reinvestment runway.
- Low multiples can reflect weak quality, not value.

## Financials Discipline

- `ROE`, book value, asset quality, capital adequacy, and payout capacity matter more than EBITDA constructs.
- Growth assumptions must be consistent with retention, capital generation, and regulation.
- A bank with poor asset quality can look optically cheap while still being overvalued.

## Growth-Company Discipline

- Revenue growth alone is not enough.
- Show the path to acceptable unit economics, margins, and funding durability.
- Use scenario-weighted valuation when the future shape of the business is uncertain.
- Funding risk and dilution are part of valuation, not side notes.

## Macro Discipline

- Higher rates raise discount rates and usually compress justified multiples.
- Highly leveraged names also suffer through earnings pressure when debt costs rise.
- Growth stocks are longer-duration assets and should be treated as more rate-sensitive.
- Country risk premium matters in Indonesia and should affect what counts as a fair multiple.

## Hard Failure Signals

- management guidance is carrying the model more than current evidence
- terminal assumptions are doing the heavy lifting
- no normalization for cyclical names
- forward estimates are used without sanity checks
- earnings are used while cash flow contradicts the profit story
- valuation ignores dilution, debt burden, or refinancing risk
- confidence is high even though valuation range is fragile
