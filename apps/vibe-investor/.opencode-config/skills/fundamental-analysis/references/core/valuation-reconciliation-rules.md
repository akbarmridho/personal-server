# Valuation Reconciliation Rules

## Purpose

Different methods should disagree sometimes. The job is not to average them blindly. The job is to explain what the disagreement is telling you about the business, the cycle, or the assumptions.

## Diagnosis Order

1. Check method fit.
2. Check cycle position and normalization.
3. Check accounting quality and cash confirmation.
4. Check capital structure and minority or holdco adjustments.
5. Check whether one method is carrying much weaker assumptions.

## Common Sources Of Divergence

| Divergence | Usual cause | What to test |
|---|---|---|
| `DCF` far above peer multiples | overly optimistic margins, growth, or terminal value | explicit assumptions, terminal share of value, peer realism |
| `P/E` looks cheap but `DCF` is weak | earnings quality issue or poor reinvestment economics | cash flow, one-offs, capex burden |
| `P/B` looks cheap but franchise value is weak | low-quality assets or unsustainable `ROE` | asset quality, capital adequacy, loss history |
| `EV/EBITDA` looks cheap but equity is not | leverage or heavy non-operating burdens | debt load, minority interest, cash needs |
| `NAV` far above market | realization discount or weak governance | debt, overhead, tax leakage, asset liquidity |
| `SOTP` far above market | complexity discount, weak disclosures, holdco burden | segment quality, debt, governance, discount justification |
| growth multiples above intrinsic output | market premium for duration or optionality | whether quality and optionality are real |

## Reconciliation Rules

- Prefer the method that best reflects the business model.
- Keep weak-fit methods as sanity checks, not anchors.
- If two methods disagree because of cycle position, normalize before concluding.
- If peer and intrinsic methods disagree, explain whether the market is paying for quality, duration, scarcity, or story.
- If disagreement remains large after adjustment, present scenarios instead of forced convergence.
- Weight by evidence quality and method fit, not by mechanical equal weighting.

## Special Cases

### Financials

- Prioritize sustainable `ROE`, book quality, capital strength, and payout capacity.
- Let near-term `P/E` move around if asset quality or provisioning is distorting earnings.

### Cyclicals

- Treat mid-cycle earnings as the real base case.
- Do not let a peak-year multiple dominate the verdict.

### Growth Names

- A wide spread between scenario outcomes is normal.
- Reconciliation should focus on what must be true operationally, not on making the numbers look neat.

### Conglomerates And Mechanisms

- Reconcile gross segment value to net equity value before comparing with market cap.
- For recap, rights issue, or dilution cases, reconcile pre-event and post-event per-share economics separately.

## Output Discipline

- State which method is primary.
- State why secondary methods are secondary.
- State the main reason methods diverge.
- State what evidence would narrow the divergence.
- If confidence is low, say so directly and widen the range.

## Hard No

- no blind averaging
- no single-number synthesis when the inputs imply a wide uncertainty band
- no premium conclusion without explaining what deserves the premium
- no cheapness conclusion when the disagreement itself points to quality risk
