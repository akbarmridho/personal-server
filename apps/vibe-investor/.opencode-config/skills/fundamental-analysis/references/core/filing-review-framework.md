# Filing Review Framework

Use this reference when `FILING_REVIEW` is active or when official disclosures materially affect a broader fundamental call.

## Selection Rules

Choose the highest-signal disclosure first:

1. Annual report or audited annual financial statements when the question is about business quality, accounting quality, governance quality, or long-cycle capital allocation.
2. Quarterly or interim financial statements when the question is about recent deterioration, inflection, or current execution.
3. Rights issue, private placement, debt issuance, major acquisition, divestment, or material transaction disclosure when the thesis depends on capital structure, control, or funding path.
4. Public expose or management presentation when the thesis depends on management claims, guidance, capex plans, or segment updates not yet visible in the statements.

Do not review a lighter document when a stronger official source is available for the same question.

## Mandatory Review Outputs

Every filing-led review must state:

- which disclosure was reviewed
- the period covered
- why this disclosure is the right primary source
- what changed versus prior understanding
- which conclusions are directly supported by the filing
- which conclusions remain inference

## Annual Report And Audited Statement Review

Mandatory checks:

- business description and segment framing still match economic reality
- revenue and margin discussion matches reported numbers
- segment and geographic mix when material
- cash flow confirms reported profit over time
- borrowings, maturity profile, and covenant or refinancing pressure
- working-capital strain, receivable or inventory buildup, and unusual prepayments
- related-party transactions and balances
- contingent liabilities, commitments, guarantees, or legal exposures
- auditor opinion and any emphasis-of-matter language
- notes-to-accounts items that materially change earnings quality, asset values, or liabilities
- management capital-allocation behavior: capex, acquisitions, dividends, buybacks, fundraising

Escalate if any of these are present:

- qualified, adverse, or disclaimer opinion
- repeated restatements
- material going-concern language
- large unexplained related-party movements
- large earnings-to-cash disconnect without clear explanation
- equity growth driven mainly by repeated capital raises rather than retained earnings

## Quarterly Or Interim Review

Mandatory checks:

- revenue, gross profit, operating profit, and net profit trajectory
- margin change and what actually drove it
- OCF versus PAT for the period and trailing view
- debt and working-capital movement
- whether the quarter reflects a real trend, a base effect, seasonality, or one-off noise

Escalate if:

- a strong headline quarter is driven mainly by one-offs
- revenue growth is not converting into cash
- margin expansion depends on temporary factors that management does not acknowledge

## Notes To Accounts

Use notes-to-accounts review when headline numbers are not enough.

Prioritize:

- revenue recognition policy
- segment definitions and changes
- related-party transactions and balances
- receivables aging, inventory detail, and impairment
- debt detail, maturities, collateral, and covenant language
- contingencies, lawsuits, tax disputes, and guarantees
- fair-value assumptions and revaluation items

Treat notes as thesis-critical when they change earnings quality, asset quality, leverage interpretation, or minority-alignment judgment.

## Auditor Remarks

Mandatory interpretation:

- opinion type
- key audit matters that matter to the thesis
- emphasis-of-matter items
- internal-control or accounting-policy warnings if disclosed

Do not treat a clean opinion as a clean business. Auditor comfort does not remove weak economics or weak governance.

## Rights Issue, Placement, Debt Raise, And Capital Structure Events

Mandatory checks:

- exact use of proceeds
- whether funds plug working-capital holes, refinance stress, or fund credible growth
- dilution magnitude and post-transaction ownership effect
- pricing quality and who participates
- whether the raise improves the balance sheet structurally or only postpones the problem

Escalate if:

- purpose is vague
- controller benefits are clearer than minority benefits
- repeated raises substitute for self-funded operations
- debt is being rolled without improving operating cash generation

## Material Transaction Review

Mandatory checks:

- what is being bought, sold, injected, or guaranteed
- strategic logic and whether it fits the core business
- valuation or pricing sanity
- leverage, dilution, or cash-flow consequences
- related-party or conflict-of-interest angle

Escalate if the transaction is highly levered, opaque, circular, or mainly cosmetic.

## Public Expose And Management Commentary

Use this as a supporting source, not as a replacement for the statements.

Mandatory checks:

- guidance credibility
- capex and funding claims
- management explanation for margin, demand, and working-capital changes
- whether commentary is consistent with the filings

Downgrade confidence when the story improves faster than the filings do.
