# Financial Statements Framework

## Fast First-Pass Screen

Before deeper ratio or note work, run this quick screen:

- asset base size and composition, especially cash, receivables, inventory, and other assets
- profit size relative to assets through ROA or ROCE as relevant
- operating cash flow positive or negative, and whether it broadly validates profit

This first pass is a filter, not the full conclusion. A name that fails here needs tighter skepticism in later sections.

## Revenue Analysis

- Start with consolidated revenue trend, then check segment and geographic mix when material.
- Separate price-led growth from volume-led growth when the business model allows it.
- Growth should reconcile with receivables, inventory, and working-capital movement.
- Check whether new growth is organic, acquisition-driven, or cyclical rebound.

## Balance Sheet Checks

### Asset alignment

- Reclassify into working capital vs fixed assets.
- Check whether composition matches industry economics.

### Liquidity and quality

- Current ratio warning when current assets < current liabilities.
- Quick ratio warning when <0.5.
- Inventory growth should not outpace profit growth.
- Receivables or inventory growth that outruns revenue needs explanation from business model and notes.
- Other assets should generally stay below 5% of total assets.

### Debt composition

- Distinguish operating payables vs interest-bearing debt.
- Track borrowings trend, maturity pressure, and refinancing dependence.
- Validate equity growth source: retained earnings vs repeated capital injections.
- Check whether reserve growth reflects real retained earnings rather than accounting noise.

## Income Statement Checks

- Confirm primary revenue driver from notes.
- Evaluate commodity sensitivity and pricing power.
- Separate fixed and variable cost pressure.
- Remove one-off gains from core earnings.
- Review segment margin mix when the business has materially different units.
- Profitability floor: NPM >2% as a practical baseline.

## Return And Capital Efficiency

| Metric | Formula | Baseline interpretation |
|------|---------|-------------------------|
| ROE | Net Income / Average Equity | High only matters if not leverage-distorted |
| ROA | Net Income / Average Assets | Useful for asset-heavy and bank-like businesses |
| ROCE | EBIT / Capital Employed | Strong read on operating capital efficiency |
| Asset Turnover | Revenue / Average Assets | Higher turnover can support lower margins |

## Cash Flow Checks

- OCF should validate accounting profit over time.
- Start with the simplest question first: is operating cash flow consistently positive when the company claims it is healthy and growing.
- OCF/Net Profit divergence is a trap signal.
- Debt repayment capacity should be realistic from OCF.
- Cash flow to debt guide: OCF/Total Debt >0.1.
- Dividend payout should be supported by FCF.
- Track CFO vs PAT over multiple years, not only one period.
- Track capex trend to separate maintenance burden from genuine expansion.

## Advanced Ratios

| Ratio | Formula | Baseline interpretation |
|------|---------|-------------------------|
| ICR | Operating Profit / Interest Expense | <1 dangerous, >3 safer |
| NGR | (Interest-Bearing Debt - Cash) / Equity | <0.5 preferred |
| Earnings Yield | EPS / Price | Compare to risk-free context |
| EV/EBITDA | EV / EBITDA | <10 often considered cheaper |

## Trend Requirement

- Minimum 3-year trend view.
- Prefer 5-10 years for cyclical businesses.
- Track revenue, margin, ROE, ROA or ROCE as relevant, cash conversion, debt path, and dividend sustainability.

## Core Formulas

- Current Ratio = Current Assets / Current Liabilities
- NPM = Net Income / Revenue
- Cash Flow to Debt = OCF / Total Debt
- FCF = OCF - CapEx
- DER = Total Debt / Total Equity
- ROE = Net Income / Average Equity
- ROA = Net Income / Average Assets
- ROCE = EBIT / Capital Employed
- Asset Turnover = Revenue / Average Assets
