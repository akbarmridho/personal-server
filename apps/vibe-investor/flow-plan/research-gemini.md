Broker-Flow Method For Indonesian Stocks1. Executive SummaryAn exhaustive analysis of market microstructure theory, algorithmic execution mechanics, and practitioner heuristics specific to the Indonesian equity market yields the following foundational directives for the design of a deterministic broker-flow analysis system:Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) Supersedes Gini: Due to the strict data contract limitation of viewing only the top 25 brokers per side, traditional concentration metrics like the Gini coefficient are mathematically compromised. The Gini index relies on the integration of the complete Lorenz curve, and truncating the "long tail" of retail brokers severely distorts the baseline. The HHI, which squares market shares, naturally neutralizes the mathematical contribution of the missing tail, making it the definitive metric for measuring sponsor concentration.Execution Quality is the Proxy for Urgency: Without access to tick-level data or order book depth, distinguishing between liquidity takers (aggressive) and liquidity providers (passive) requires a daily benchmark proxy. Comparing a top broker's average execution price against the daily session Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP) robustly isolates execution urgency. Brokers buying above the daily VWAP exhibit aggressive accumulation, signaling high conviction.Order Splitting Drives Predictable Persistence: Institutional market participants minimize slippage by slicing large parent orders into smaller child orders over consecutive trading sessions. This generates statistically significant autocorrelation in order flow. Measuring broker persistence across a primary 30-day window captures this institutional footprint and separates it from random retail noise.Spearman Rank IC Trumps Pearson Correlation: To establish the trustworthiness of flow signals relative to price action, the Spearman Rank Information Coefficient (IC) is vastly superior to the Pearson correlation. Financial time series, particularly in emerging markets, feature extreme volume and price outliers that skew linear relationships. Spearman evaluates monotonic relationships via rank-ordering, ensuring that single-day volume anomalies do not destroy the correlation metric.Wash Risk Requires Heuristic Proxies: Absolute detection of wash trading requires matching trade IDs on a tick-by-tick basis. However, a deterministic proxy is highly effective for V1: identifying brokers who appear symmetrically on both the top 25 buy and sell lists with near-identical volumes and execution prices within the same session. Flow from these brokers must be heavily discounted.Net Flow Must Be Approximated from Gross: The Cumulative Accumulation Distribution Index (CADI) relies on net flow, yet the raw data contract provides gross top-25 lists. Calculating net flow requires cross-referencing the buy and sell lists and assigning a zero-value (or inferred threshold value) to the missing side, accepting a calculable margin of error for brokers falling outside the visible top 25.Regimes Dictate Trust: Broker flow analysis is not universally applicable. Micro-cap and low-liquidity equities are highly susceptible to single-actor manipulation, rendering dispersion metrics volatile and unreliable. A strict regime-filtering matrix based on market capitalization and average daily traded value must dictate the degree of trust applied to any flow verdict.Divergence Contextualizes, Never Triggers: Consistent with the product doctrine, broker flow can lead price action , but divergence serves exclusively as contextual setup. Flow analysis describes conditions of "warning" or "lead," leaving trade execution, stop placement, and target ladders entirely to technical price-action analysis.Deferred Concepts: The Gross Volume Participation Ratio (GVPR) requires precise total market volume matching , and "Smart Money Tracker" (SMT) style composite scores are largely ambiguous retail heuristics. Exact implementations of these concepts should be deferred until a strict proxy matrix is established or complete broker ledgers become available.2. Market Microstructure and Theoretical FormulationsThe Indonesian stock market presents a unique structural environment. The Indonesia Stock Exchange (IDX) provides daily broker summary data, revealing the gross volume, value, and average execution price of the top participating brokerage houses. While recent regulatory changes have obscured real-time broker IDs during the intraday session to curb retail herding, the end-of-day summaries remain intact. This structural transparency allows for the quantitative tracking of institutional capital flow—often referred to locally by practitioners as "Bandarmology".To elevate this localized framework into a robust, deterministic system, the analysis must translate raw gross data into standard market microstructure metrics. The following sections address the critical research parameters necessary for system implementation.2.1. CADI (Cumulative Accumulation Distribution Index)CADI is not a globally standardized academic metric; rather, it is a product-specific heuristic deeply embedded in Indonesian equity analysis. It serves as a continuous running total of net institutional money flow. In practice, practitioners compute CADI by subtracting the volume of the top net sellers from the top net buyers.Because the provided data contract supplies only the top 25 gross buyers and sellers, exact net flow is mathematically obscured. If a broker purchases 50,000 lots (placing them in the top 25 buyers) but sells 5,000 lots (placing them 40th on the sell list, thus invisible), an exact net calculation is impossible. The most defensible approximation for a deterministic V1 system is to cross-reference the two lists. If a broker appears on the top 25 buy list but not the top 25 sell list, the system must assign a default sell value of zero, treating the gross buy volume as the net buy volume. While this introduces a slight upward bias, the error margin is strictly bounded by the volume of the 25th broker on the opposing list. Over a rolling window, this noise normalizes, effectively capturing the dominant directional flow.The trend of CADI should be computed not by raw values, but by plotting the CADI series against a simple moving average (SMA) of itself, or by calculating the linear regression slope over the primary window.2.2. Broker-Side VWAP and Execution UrgencyThe Volume-Weighted Average Price (VWAP) is the canonical benchmark utilized by institutional trading desks to evaluate execution quality. The objective of algorithmic execution is to slice large orders to match or beat the market VWAP, thereby minimizing slippage and market impact.Without access to tick-by-tick tape data, classifying trades strictly as liquidity-taking (aggressive) or liquidity-providing (passive) is impossible. However, the top-25 daily broker summary provides each broker's average execution price. Comparing a specific top broker's average price against the consolidated daily session VWAP yields a highly robust proxy for urgency:Aggressive Accumulation: If a top net-buying broker's average execution price is significantly higher than the daily session VWAP, the broker is consistently crossing the bid-ask spread to take liquidity. This premium payment indicates high urgency, signaling strong institutional conviction or a mandate to secure inventory regardless of short-term cost.Passive Accumulation: If a top net-buying broker's average execution price is below the daily VWAP, the broker is utilizing resting limit orders to absorb selling pressure. This suggests "stealth" accumulation, typically occurring at support levels.Aggressive Distribution: Conversely, a top net-selling broker whose average price is below the daily VWAP is hitting the bid to liquidate inventory, signaling a lack of price sensitivity and high bearish conviction.The session VWAP (calculated from the daily OHLCV as Total Value / Total Volume) is the only mathematically pure denominator. Using a selected-window VWAP introduces arbitrary anchoring biases. Thresholds for aggressive versus passive should be dynamic, but a baseline of $\pm$ 10 to 15 basis points deviation from the daily VWAP is a standard institutional benchmark for defining significant variance.2.3. GVPR (Gross Volume Participation Ratio)Gross Volume Participation Ratio (GVPR) assesses the footprint of specific market participants relative to the total market volume. For the V1 system, computing the GVPR of the top 25 brokers involves summing their gross traded volume and dividing it by twice the total market volume (to account for the fact that every trade has a buyer and a seller).While GVPR provides context regarding market dominance, it carries a severe structural bias when reliant on truncated top-25 data. In highly illiquid micro-cap stocks, the top 25 brokers may account for 95% of the total volume. In highly liquid large-cap stocks, retail fragmentation means the top 25 brokers might only account for 35% of the volume. Consequently, GVPR should be utilized strictly as a contextual filter to determine how much of the total market action is visible to the system, rather than as an isolated directional signal.2.4. Smart-Money Composite (SMT)"Smart Money Concepts" (SMC) or "Smart Money Tracker" (SMT) are heavily marketed retail heuristics rather than standardized academic metrics. Attempting to build an exact "SMT score" often leads to proprietary black-box calculations that violate the deterministic transparency required for this system.Instead of a nebulous SMT score, the system should rely on a "Sponsor Quality" classification. The most defensible components of institutional footprinting include concentration (HHI), execution urgency (VWAP variance), and persistence (multi-day streaks). Institutions leave these specific footprints because of the mathematical constraints of deploying large capital without causing severe market impact. A robust system presents these components cleanly rather than blending them into an arbitrary composite.2.5. PersistenceOrder flow persistence—the positive autocorrelation of buy or sell orders—is a universally recognized phenomenon in market microstructure. Academic literature, notably Lillo and Farmer (2004) and Toth et al. (2011), demonstrates that this persistence is overwhelmingly driven by order splitting rather than herd behavior. A single large institution slices a parent order over days or weeks, resulting in the same broker appearing consistently on one side of the ledger.Persistence should be measured via consecutive same-side streaks. However, a simple streak count is vulnerable to noise from minor brokers. To sanitize the metric, persistence must be weighted by traded value. A 5-day streak by a broker accumulating Rp 50 Billion carries infinitely more weight than a 5-day streak by a retail broker accumulating Rp 50 Million. Furthermore, the metric must aggressively degrade if the broker exhibits gross crossing (appearing simultaneously as a top buyer and top seller), as this indicates market-making or wash-trading rather than directional sponsorship.2.6. Concentration: HHI vs. GiniDetermining the concentration of market participants is vital for separating coordinated institutional action from scattered retail noise.The Gini coefficient is the standard econometric measure for inequality, calculated via the area under the Lorenz curve. However, the Gini coefficient requires the complete population distribution. Because the available data contract truncates at the top 25 brokers, the bottom 70% to 90% of the broker population is missing. Attempting to calculate a Gini index on a truncated dataset mathematically shifts the Lorenz curve unpredictably, destroying the metric's baseline and rendering cross-asset comparisons invalid.The Herfindahl-Hirschman Index (HHI) is the superior, academically robust alternative for truncated data. The HHI is calculated by squaring the market share of each participant ($\sum s_i^2$). The act of squaring inherently minimizes the mathematical contribution of small participants. A broker with a 0.5% market share contributes only 0.25 points to the HHI, whereas a broker with a 20% market share contributes 400 points. Therefore, the missing "long tail" of brokers beyond the top 25 is statistically irrelevant to the final HHI score.Credible thresholds, adapted from standard antitrust definitions, are:HHI < 1,500: Unconcentrated (Balanced, highly fragmented retail participation).HHI 1,500 - 2,500: Moderately concentrated.HHI > 2,500: Highly concentrated (Heavy, coordinated sponsorship, often termed "Bandar accumulation").2.7. Flow-Price CorrelationDetermining the relationship between broker flow and price action dictates how much trust the system places in the flow data. The optimal methodology pairs the daily closing price series against the daily CADI (net flow) series.The Pearson correlation coefficient measures strict linear relationships. However, financial time series are notoriously noisy and non-linear, prone to extreme single-day volume spikes that can entirely warp a linear calculation.The Spearman Rank Information Coefficient (IC) is the definitive standard for quantitative equity evaluation. Spearman converts the raw values of price and CADI into ranks (e.g., from 1 to 30 over a 30-day window) and correlates the ranks. This neutralizes extreme volume outliers while perfectly capturing monotonic relationships. If flow consistently increases alongside price, the Spearman Rank IC will approach +1.0, regardless of whether the magnitude of the daily increases is perfectly linear.2.8. DivergenceDivergence compares the structural trajectory of price against the underlying broker flow, serving strictly as contextual setup rather than an execution trigger.Bullish Divergence: Defined as price making lower lows or consolidating, while the primary CADI window exhibits higher lows or the buy-side HHI spikes significantly. This indicates that institutional sponsors are quietly absorbing supply while retail panic drives the price down. It constitutes a warning for short positions and a lead context for long setups.Bearish Divergence: Defined as price making higher highs while CADI makes lower highs or sell-side HHI increases. This signifies distribution into strength; retail momentum is pushing the price upward while major sponsors use the liquidity to exit their positions.Divergence requires a sufficient sample size to mitigate false signals. A minimum of 10 to 15 trading sessions is required before a divergence structure can be considered statistically meaningful.2.9. Wash Risk and Anomaly ProxiesWash trading involves simultaneous buying and selling by the same entity to generate artificial volume, a practice highly detrimental to flow analysis. Absolute detection requires matching specific trade IDs across the exchange matching engine.Given the constraints of daily top-25 summaries, a highly effective deterministic proxy can be established by analyzing topological symmetry. If a specific broker code (e.g., YP or MG) appears as a top 5 buyer and a top 5 seller on the same day, the system evaluates:Volume Symmetry: Is the buy volume within 2-5% of the sell volume?Price Symmetry: Is the average buy execution price virtually identical to the average sell execution price?If both conditions are met, the probability of internalized cross-trading or wash trading is critically high. For V1 implementation, the net flow from brokers triggering this anomaly proxy must be aggressively discounted or entirely nullified from the CADI calculation to prevent false accumulation signals.2.10. Sponsor QualitySponsor quality separates robust institutional flow from erratic retail noise. It is not a single variable but a combinatory interpretation of the metrics defined above.Strong Sponsor: Characterized by high buy-side concentration (HHI > 2,500), multi-day persistence streaks, and aggressive execution (paying a premium above the daily VWAP).Constructive Sponsor: Moderate HHI, steady positive CADI slope, passive execution (absorbing supply below VWAP).Mixed / Noisy: Low HHI (< 1,500), broken persistence streaks, and negative or volatile Spearman Rank IC. Flow in this state is dominated by fragmented retail action and provides no predictive edge.Weak Sponsor (Active Distribution): High sell-side HHI combined with aggressive selling below the daily VWAP.2.11. Trust RegimeFlow analysis must be dynamically weighted by the macroeconomic reality of the specific equity.High Liquidity / Large Cap: Institutional order splitting is the dominant driver of flow. CADI, VWAP execution, and persistence are highly reliable. The flow acts primarily as support_only or confirm, as these stocks are heavily arbitraged and macro-driven.Medium/Low Liquidity / Mid/Small Cap: This is the optimal regime for flow analysis. A single strong sponsor can dictate the entire trend. Concentration metrics (HHI) yield massive predictive value. Flow in this regime is heavily lead_capable.Very Low Liquidity / Micro Cap: Flow data is highly susceptible to erratic manipulation and wash trading. A single day's volume can warp a 30-day CADI baseline. Flow signals in this regime must be flagged as unreliable.2.12. Window ChoiceThe selection of the primary lookback window is critical for aligning the system with actual institutional behavior.30 Trading Days (Primary): A 30-trading-day window (roughly six calendar weeks) is the optimal primary default. Institutional VWAP and TWAP algorithmic mandates are frequently bound to monthly or slightly longer execution cycles. A 30-day window provides a sufficient $n=30$ sample size to calculate a statistically robust Spearman Rank IC without introducing the excessive historical drag that plagues longer moving averages.60 Trading Days (Secondary): A 60-day window (approximately one financial quarter) is necessary exclusively for establishing macroeconomic divergence and evaluating the baseline regime. Comparing the 30-day CADI slope against the 60-day CADI slope reveals whether current accumulation is a short-term correction or a macro-structural shift.3. Formula NotesThe following formulas represent the deterministic mathematical implementation required for the V1 system, accounting for the constraints of the daily top-25 data contract.4.1. CADI (Approximate Net Flow)To resolve the gross-to-net truncation issue, the system iterates through the top $k$ brokers (default $k=5$ to $10$) to establish daily net volume.For each trading day $t$:Let $B$ be the set of Top $k$ Buyers and $S$ be the set of Top $k$ Sellers.For each broker $i \in (B \cup S)$:$$NetVol_{i,t} = BuyVol_{i,t} - SellVol_{i,t}$$(If broker $i$ is not present on one side of the top-25 list, that side's volume evaluates to 0).Calculate Daily CADI:$$DailyCADI_t = \sum_{i \in B} NetVol_{i,t} + \sum_{j \in S} NetVol_{j,t}$$Calculate Rolling CADI (Lookback $w=30$):$$CADI_{w} = \sum_{t=1}^{w} DailyCADI_t$$4.2. Broker-Side VWAP Execution QualityThis algorithm evaluates execution urgency relative to the daily market mean.Calculate Market VWAP for day $t$:$$VWAP_{m,t} = \frac{OHLCV\_Value_t}{OHLCV\_Volume_t}$$For top net-buying broker $i$, calculate Execution Premium in basis points:$$Exec\_bps_{i,t} = \left( \frac{BuyAvgPrice_{i,t} - VWAP_{m,t}}{VWAP_{m,t}} \right) \times 10,000$$Thresholds:$Exec\_bps > +10$: Aggressive Accumulation (Taking liquidity).$Exec\_bps < -10$: Passive Accumulation (Providing liquidity).4.3. HHI ConcentrationHHI minimizes the mathematical impact of the missing broker tail.For the buy side on day $t$, calculate the relative share $s_i$ for each of the 25 visible brokers:$$s_{i,t} = \frac{BuyVol_{i,t}}{\sum_{k=1}^{25} BuyVol_{k,t}}$$Calculate Buy HHI:$$HHI_{buy,t} = \sum_{i=1}^{25} (s_{i,t})^2 \times 10,000$$Smooth over 5-day SMA to prevent single-day block trade distortions.4.4. Flow-Price Correlation (Spearman Rank IC)Evaluates the monotonic alignment between Price and Flow over window $n=30$.Rank the daily Closing Prices $P_{t}$ from 1 to $n$. Let this rank array be $R_P$.Rank the daily $CADI_{t}$ values from 1 to $n$. Let this rank array be $R_C$.For each day $t$, calculate the rank difference $d_t = R_{P,t} - R_{C,t}$.Compute Spearman $\rho$:$$\rho = 1 - \frac{6 \sum_{t=1}^{n} d_t^2}{n(n^2 - 1)}$$Interpretation: $\rho > 0.6$ indicates a strong, trustworthy monotonic relationship where flow leads or confirms price. $\rho < 0$ indicates severe divergence or noisy, fragmented flow.4.5. Wash Risk Anomaly ProxyActs as a daily sanitization filter before CADI computation.For any broker $i$ present on both the Top 25 Buy and Sell lists on day $t$:Volume Symmetry:$$V_{sym} = 1 - \frac{|BuyVol_{i,t} - SellVol_{i,t}|}{BuyVol_{i,t} + SellVol_{i,t}}$$Price Symmetry:$$P_{sym} = 1 - \frac{|BuyAvg_{i,t} - SellAvg_{i,t}|}{VWAP_{m,t}}$$Action: If $V_{sym} > 0.90$ AND $P_{sym} > 0.99$, flag broker $i$ as High Wash Risk. Set $NetVol_{i,t} = 0$ to prevent artificial CADI inflation.5. Window RecommendationPrimary Window: 30 Trading Days
A 30-trading-day window (encompassing roughly six calendar weeks) is recommended as the definitive default for V1. Institutional accumulation and distribution phases, governed by algorithmic TWAP and VWAP constraints, are heavily tied to monthly performance reporting, rebalancing, and fund deployment cycles. A 30-day window guarantees the capture of at least one full institutional cycle. Furthermore, statistical measurements like the Spearman Rank IC require a sample size of at least $n=30$ to ensure statistical significance and minimize the impact of day-to-day retail noise.Secondary Window: 60 Trading DaysA 60-day window (one financial quarter) should be utilized exclusively as a slow baseline to establish the macro divergence regime. By plotting the 30-day CADI against the 60-day CADI, the system can instantly determine if recent flow represents a short-term tactical correction or a fundamental structural shift in sponsor positioning.6. The missing long tail of the distribution permanently corrupts the Lorenz curve integration.SMT (Smart Money Tracker) Composite Score: Fusing independent variables (HHI, CADI, VWAP) into a single overarching score requires arbitrary, non-academic weightings. The system should present the discrete metrics individually rather than synthesizing a "black box" score.Exact GVPR: True participation ratios require absolute market volume matching. The top-25 proxy is acceptable for macro context but lacks the precision required for a core V1 metric.Taker/Maker Tick Classification: Strictly impossible without order-book depth and tick-level trade IDs. The VWAP Execution Quality formula completely replaces this requirement for V1.8.
